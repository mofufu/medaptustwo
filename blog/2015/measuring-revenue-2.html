The Top Ten Thanksgiving Mishaps via ICD-10

November 20, 2015 | Jen Crowley - Home / Blog

If you read our blog and follow our news stream, you know that ICD-10 is a topic that is near and dear to hearts and those of our many customers and end-users. While there remains much to be seen and known around the actual impact of ICD-10 on denials, it’s hard to disagree with the beauty of ICD-10: if it’s a malady, there’s a code for it. As we head into Thanksgiving week, a holiday wrought with personal dangers that could lead to an ER visit, it seemed like a fun idea to code what we think are…

The Top Ten Thanksgiving Mishaps via ICD-10

10. For some people, a frozen turkey simply doesn’t cut it. Some turkeys aren’t afraid to fight back: W61.43XA, pecked by a turkey

9. Family gatherings can be stressful but be sure not to imbibe too much – it may dull the pain now but what about later tonight: W06.XXXA, fall from bed

8. Flight turbulence, long lines of traffic, rushing to get home to change into bigger pants – it’s enough to make a person woozy: T75.3, motion sickness

7. For some other people, an old fashioned oven baked turkey isn’t enough. Some turkey fryers aren’t afraid to fight back: T22.1, burn

6. Family drama – need I say more: F43.0, stress reaction

5. It’s a mild day and a quiet morning, why not hang some festive holiday lights? Black ice, that’s why: W11.XXXA, fall from ladder

4. Live in a snow belt and want to take your cousin from Florida out on a snow machine for a spin? Have him watch his step: V86.42XA, injury while boarding a snowmobile

3. Sure you haven’t exercised in awhile but a quick game of football before dinner sure sounds like fun: S83.231A, meniscus tear

2. Who can resist Aunt Ellen’s famous peanut brittle? S02.5XXA, broken tooth

1. You knew this was coming… turkey juice on your countertop: O157.H7, food poisoning

Hope this gave you a laugh and that you have much to be thankful for this year!
